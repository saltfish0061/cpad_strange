1. Background of the Case Study

The selected case study is ???, a campus-based food service provider that offers a variety of meals and beverages to students and staff. Currently, the cafeteria operates primarily through walk-in orders, where customers queue at different stalls to place their orders. Some vendors also accept orders via messaging applications such as WhatsApp during peak hours.

However, this approach is not centralized, and each vendor manages orders independently. Customers often need to physically visit the cafeteria to check menu availability and place orders, which can be inconvenient, especially during busy periods.


2. Problem Statement

The current ordering process at ??? faces several issues:

- Long waiting time during peak hours due to manual order processing and queues.
- Lack of centralized system, causing inefficiency as each stall handles orders separately.
- Limited accessibility, where customers must be physically present or rely on informal messaging methods.
- Order inaccuracies, especially when handling multiple items or large orders manually.
- No proper record management, making it difficult for vendors to track sales and analyze demand.

These challenges reduce operational efficiency and negatively impact customer experience.


3. Proposed Solution

To address these issues, a web-based Online Food & Drink Ordering System is proposed. The system will allow customers to browse menus, select multiple food and beverage items, and place orders online through desktop or mobile devices.

The system will provide a centralized platform where all cafeteria vendors can manage their menus and orders efficiently. Customers will be able to place orders in advance, reducing waiting time and improving convenience. Additionally, the system will automatically record order data, helping vendors manage and analyze their business operations.


4. Project Scope

The proposed system will include the following functionalities:

Customer Module:
- Browse menu items by vendor
- Add multiple items to cart and place orders
- View order summary and status

Vendor/Staff Module:
- Manage menu items (add, update, delete)
- View and process incoming orders
- Admin Module:
- Manage users and vendors
- Monitor system operations

The system will be accessible via web browsers on both desktop and mobile devices.


5. Objectives of the Project

The objectives of this project are:

- To analyze the current ordering process and identify its limitations.
- To design a web-based system architecture and database for managing online food and drink orders.
- To develop an Online Food & Drink Ordering System that supports multiple menu item selection and centralized order management.
- To implement and test the system to ensure it meets functional and usability requirements.
